Fund Administrator Job Salary In The Uk: What You Need To Know In 2023

Introduction

If you're considering a career as a fund administrator in the UK, you're probably wondering how much you can expect to earn. In this article, we'll take a look at the current job market and provide you with an overview of fund administrator job salaries in the UK.

What is a Fund Administrator?

Before we dive into salary information, let's first define what a fund administrator does. Fund administrators are responsible for managing the administrative tasks associated with investment funds, such as hedge funds or mutual funds. They handle tasks like record-keeping, reporting, and compliance.

Current Job Market

The fund administration industry in the UK is currently experiencing growth, with more and more investment funds being established every year. This has created a high demand for skilled fund administrators.

Entry-Level Salaries

If you're just starting out as a fund administrator, you can expect to earn a starting salary of around £20,000 to £25,000 per year. This will vary depending on the size of the investment firm and your level of experience.

Mid-Level Salaries

As you gain more experience in the field, your salary will increase. Mid-level fund administrators can expect to earn between £30,000 and £40,000 per year.

Senior-Level Salaries

Senior fund administrators with several years of experience can earn upwards of £50,000 per year. Those in management positions can earn even more.

Factors That Affect Salary

Several factors can affect your fund administrator job salary in the UK. These include:

Experience

As mentioned earlier, your level of experience will play a significant role in determining your salary. The more experience you have, the higher your salary will be.

Location

Salaries can vary depending on where you work in the UK. Typically, salaries are higher in London compared to other parts of the country.

Qualifications

Having relevant qualifications, such as a degree in finance or accounting, can increase your earning potential.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the fund administration industry in the UK is growing, and there is a high demand for skilled professionals. While entry-level salaries may not be high, there is plenty of room for growth and advancement. Keep in mind that factors such as experience, location, and qualifications can all affect your salary. As you gain more experience and take on more responsibilities, you can expect your salary to increase accordingly.
