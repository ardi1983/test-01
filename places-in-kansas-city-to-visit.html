The City of Fountains

If you're planning a trip to Kansas City in 2023, you're in for a treat! This Midwestern city is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture. But what sets Kansas City apart from other cities is its nickname - the City of Fountains. With over 200 fountains, you're never too far from a beautiful water display. Here are our top 10 places to visit in Kansas City.

1. The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art

The Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art is a must-visit for any art lover. This museum boasts an impressive collection of over 35,000 works of art, ranging from ancient to contemporary. Be sure to check out the famous Shuttlecocks sculptures on the lawn!

2. The Country Club Plaza

The Country Club Plaza is a historic shopping district that features Spanish-inspired architecture, fountains, and beautiful landscaping. It's the perfect place to spend an afternoon shopping, dining, or just strolling.

3. Union Station

Union Station is a historic train station that has been transformed into a cultural hub. It features a science center, a planetarium, and multiple exhibits. Don't miss the outdoor concert series in the summer!

4. Kauffman Center for the Performing Arts

The Kauffman Center for the Performing Arts is a stunning architectural masterpiece that hosts a variety of concerts, plays, and performances. Be sure to check out the view from the rooftop terrace!

5. The National World War I Museum and Memorial

The National World War I Museum and Memorial is a must-visit for history buffs. It features exhibits and artifacts from the Great War, as well as a 217-foot tall tower with stunning views of the city.

6. The Arabia Steamboat Museum

The Arabia Steamboat Museum is a unique museum that features artifacts from a steamboat that sank in the Missouri River in 1856. The museum offers a fascinating glimpse into life in the 19th century.

7. The Kemper Museum of Contemporary Art

The Kemper Museum of Contemporary Art is a free museum that features rotating exhibitions of modern art. It's a great place to discover new artists and explore the world of contemporary art.

8. Loose Park

Loose Park is a beautiful park that features a rose garden, a lake, and plenty of walking trails. It's the perfect place to relax and enjoy nature.

9. The Negro Leagues Baseball Museum

The Negro Leagues Baseball Museum is a unique museum that celebrates the history of African American baseball. It features exhibits and artifacts from the Negro Leagues, as well as interactive exhibits.

10. The American Jazz Museum

Kansas City is known for its rich jazz history, and the American Jazz Museum celebrates that history. It features exhibits, performances, and educational programs that explore the world of jazz.

Conclusion

There you have it - our top 10 places to visit in Kansas City in 2023. Whether you're a history buff, an art lover, or just looking for a fun day out, Kansas City has something for everyone. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your trip today!
