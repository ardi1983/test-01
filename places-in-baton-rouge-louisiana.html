Discovering The Best Places In Baton Rouge, Louisiana

Introduction

Baton Rouge, Louisiana, is a vibrant city with a rich history and culture that every visitor should experience. From its stunning architecture to its mouth-watering cuisine, there are plenty of reasons why Baton Rouge is a popular tourist destination. In this article, we will explore the top 10 places to visit in Baton Rouge, so sit back, relax, and enjoy the ride.

1. Louisiana State Capitol Building

The Louisiana State Capitol Building is a must-visit destination in Baton Rouge. This stunning building, which is the tallest capitol building in the US, is made of white limestone and features a breathtaking art deco design. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and explore the various exhibits and displays that showcase the state's history.

2. USS Kidd

The USS Kidd is a historic naval ship that is now a museum in Baton Rouge. The ship, which was named after Rear Admiral Isaac C. Kidd, who died during the attack on Pearl Harbor, is a fascinating place to visit. Visitors can explore the ship and learn about its history, as well as enjoy the stunning views of the Mississippi River from the deck.

3. Louisiana Art & Science Museum

If you're a fan of art and science, then the Louisiana Art & Science Museum is the place for you. This museum features a range of exhibits and displays that explore the intersection of art and science. From the planetarium to the art galleries, there's something for everyone at this unique museum.

4. Shaw Center for the Arts

The Shaw Center for the Arts is a state-of-the-art performing arts center in Baton Rouge. This stunning building features multiple theaters and performance spaces, as well as art galleries and a rooftop terrace with breathtaking views of the city. Whether you're interested in theater, dance, music, or art, the Shaw Center has something for everyone.

5. LSU Rural Life Museum

The LSU Rural Life Museum is a living history museum that showcases Louisiana's rural life from the 18th and 19th centuries. Visitors can explore the various buildings on the museum grounds, such as the plantation homes, barns, and blacksmith shops, and learn about the daily lives of Louisiana's early settlers.

6. Bluebonnet Swamp Nature Center

If you're looking to escape the hustle and bustle of the city, then the Bluebonnet Swamp Nature Center is the perfect place to visit. This nature center features a range of trails and boardwalks that allow visitors to explore the stunning natural beauty of Louisiana's wetlands. From birdwatching to hiking, there's plenty to do at this peaceful nature reserve.

7. Louisiana Old State Capitol

The Louisiana Old State Capitol is another historic building in Baton Rouge that is worth a visit. This stunning Gothic Revival building served as the Louisiana State Capitol from 1852 to 1932 and now serves as a museum. Visitors can explore the various exhibits and displays that showcase Louisiana's political history.

8. Baton Rouge Zoo

The Baton Rouge Zoo is a popular destination for families with children. This zoo features a range of animals, including tigers, giraffes, and elephants, as well as a petting zoo and a playground. There's plenty to see and do at this fun-filled zoo.

9. Louisiana State University

Louisiana State University is a top-ranked public research university located in Baton Rouge. Visitors can explore the beautiful campus and take in the stunning architecture, as well as attend a sporting event or cultural performance. Whether you're a student, alumni, or just a curious visitor, LSU is a must-visit destination in Baton Rouge.

10. Baton Rouge River Center

The Baton Rouge River Center is a multipurpose arena that hosts a range of events, from concerts to sporting events. If you're looking for some entertainment during your visit to Baton Rouge, then the River Center is the place to be. Check out the schedule of events to see what's happening during your visit.

Conclusion

Baton Rouge, Louisiana, is a city with a rich culture and history that every visitor should experience. From the stunning architecture to the mouth-watering cuisine, there's something for everyone in this vibrant city. Whether you're interested in history, art, nature, or entertainment, Baton Rouge has it all. So what are you waiting for? Plan your trip to Baton Rouge today and discover all that this amazing city has to offer.
