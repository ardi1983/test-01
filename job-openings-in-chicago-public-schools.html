Introduction

Chicago Public Schools (CPS) is one of the largest school districts in the United States, serving over 350,000 students across more than 600 schools. As such, it offers a wide variety of job openings, from teaching positions to administrative roles. In this article, we'll take a closer look at some of the job opportunities available in CPS.

Teaching Positions

CPS is always on the lookout for talented and dedicated teachers who are passionate about helping students succeed. Whether you're an experienced educator or just starting out, there are a variety of teaching positions available in CPS. Some of the most common roles include elementary school teacher, middle school teacher, and high school teacher.

Elementary School Teacher

As an elementary school teacher in CPS, you'll be responsible for teaching a wide range of subjects to students in grades K-5. This includes math, science, social studies, and language arts. You'll also be expected to develop lesson plans, grade assignments, and communicate with parents.

Middle School Teacher

Middle school teachers in CPS typically work with students in grades 6-8. You'll be responsible for teaching a variety of subjects, including math, science, social studies, and language arts. You'll also be expected to develop lesson plans, grade assignments, and communicate with parents.

High School Teacher

As a high school teacher in CPS, you'll be responsible for teaching a specific subject to students in grades 9-12. This could include English, math, science, social studies, or a foreign language. You'll also be expected to develop lesson plans, grade assignments, and communicate with parents.

Administrative Roles

In addition to teaching positions, CPS also offers a variety of administrative roles. These positions are typically responsible for managing various aspects of the school district, such as finances, human resources, and facilities.

Finance Manager

As a finance manager in CPS, you'll be responsible for managing the district's finances. This includes developing budgets, tracking expenses, and ensuring that funds are allocated appropriately. You'll also be expected to communicate financial information to other members of the district, such as school principals and central office staff.

Human Resources Manager

As a human resources manager in CPS, you'll be responsible for managing the district's talent. This includes recruiting, hiring, and training new employees, as well as managing employee benefits and ensuring that the district is in compliance with labor laws.

Facilities Manager

As a facilities manager in CPS, you'll be responsible for managing the district's buildings and grounds. This includes overseeing maintenance and repairs, managing construction projects, and ensuring that buildings are clean and safe for students and staff.

Conclusion

Whether you're interested in teaching or administrative roles, there are plenty of job opportunities available in CPS. By joining the CPS team, you'll have the opportunity to make a difference in the lives of thousands of students and help them achieve their full potential. So if you're looking for a rewarding career in education, be sure to check out the job openings in Chicago Public Schools!
